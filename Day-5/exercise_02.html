Nikola Tesla: The Genius Who Electrified the World

Early Life

Born: July 10, 1856
Place: Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)

Nikola Tesla was born into a Serbian family. His father was a priest and a writer, while his mother, though unschooled, was highly inventive. From an early age, Tesla showed an extraordinary ability to memorize books and solve complex mathematical problems in his head.

Education and Early Life

Tesla sudied electrical engineering at the Austrian Polytechnic but never completer his degree. He later worked for Edison's Company in Paris, where he gained pracical experience with electrical systems. In 1884, he immigrated to United States to work under Thomas Edison, but their differing views on electricity soon led to a rivalry.

Inventions and Contributions

Tesla is best known for his work on Alternating Current (AC), which became the stadard for electrical power distribution. Some of his most notable inventions and Contributions include:

  • AC Power System - A more efficient electrical system than Edison's direct current (DC).
  • Tesla Coil - A high-voltage transfromer still used in wireless technology.
  • Radio Waves - Pioneered wireless communication before Marconi.
  • X-Rays - Conducted early experiments with radiation.
  • Wireless Electricity - Dreamed of transmitting electricity without wires, leading to the Wardenclyffe Town project.

Later Years and Legacy

Despite his brilliance, Tesla struggled financially in his later years. He lived in a hotel in New york, often feeding pigeons and working on secret inventions. He passed away on January 7, 1943, alone but respected as one of the greatest inventions in hsitory.

Today, his contributions power the modern world, and he is recognised as visionary ahead of his time. Companies like Tesla, Inc. (Founded by Elon Musk) carry his legay forward.
